An annuity is a contract with an insurance company that helps you grow and protect your money. You contribute funds (either a lump sum or over time), and in return you receive guaranteed growth at a fixed rate, principal protection, and the option for future income. Fixed annuities are similar to CDs but often offer higher rates and tax-deferred growth.

Annuities can be part of a savings strategy at many life stages. Here’s a general guide:

  • 20s & 25s: Good for building a guaranteed-growth foundation alongside other investments. Fixed annuities offer principal protection and competitive rates, useful if you want a portion of savings with no market risk.
  • 30s: Well-suited for mid-term goals (e.g., 5–7 years). You can lock in rates and benefit from tax-deferred growth while keeping flexibility for future needs.
  • 35+: Often ideal for balancing risk as you accumulate more assets. Fixed annuities can anchor your portfolio with predictable growth and help protect what you’ve saved.

Your situation is unique. Consider your goals, timeline, and risk tolerance. A financial professional can help you decide if an annuity fits your plan.
