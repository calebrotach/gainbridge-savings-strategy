Savings Pulse: the journey from ideation to a successful implementation

A strategic framework for defined success, phased rollouts, and friction-free user experience.

The Challenge

"You are launching a new savings feature. How would you define success, structure the rollout, and ensure it's a simple UX?"

Prompt Components

The challenge breaks down into three core questions we address in this framework:

1

Define Success

What metrics and outcomes signal that the launch is working? North star, engagement, and efficiency indicators.

2

Structure the Rollout

How do we phase the launch to de-risk and validate? Alpha, Beta, and General Availability stages.

3

Ensure a Simple UX

What principles keep the experience friction-free and user-centric? Progressive disclosure, one-click flows, and clear feedback.

1. Define Success

North Star

Net New Inflow / AUM

  • New deposits attributed to Savings Pulse
  • Growth in assets under management from savings flows
  • Primary indicator of business impact

Engagement

Day-30 Retention

  • % of users who funded and remain active at 30 days
  • Signals product stickiness and habit formation
  • Target threshold for Beta → GA promotion

Efficiency

Time-to-Value (TTV)

  • Time from first touch to first funded account
  • Lower TTV = less friction, higher conversion
  • Acceptable limits vary by target segment

2. Structure the Rollout

Alpha

Internal / Closed Beta: Focus on edge-case failures and regulatory blocks.

  • Internal testing checklist covering all flows (goal, term, link bank, fund) — QA, Engineering, Product
  • Edge-case and failure-mode documentation with remediation plan — QA, Engineering
  • Compliance and regulatory review with legal sign-off — Legal, Compliance
  • Feedback log from internal stakeholders and QA — Product, QA, Design
  • Go/no-go criteria and readiness checklist for Beta — Product, Engineering, Legal, QA
Beta

Segmented Cohort: A/B testing messaging and value props.

  • Cohort selection criteria and invitation/onboarding flow — Growth, Product, Marketing
  • A/B test framework with messaging and value-prop variants — Analytics, Product, Marketing
  • Analytics and conversion tracking (funnel, drop-off, TTV) — Analytics, Engineering
  • Qualitative feedback collection (surveys, interviews) — Product, Design, UX Research
  • Beta report with recommendations for GA messaging and UX — Product, Analytics, Design
GA

General Availability: Operational scale and support readiness.

  • Load and scale testing with projected traffic volumes — Engineering, DevOps/SRE
  • Support documentation, FAQs, and agent training materials — Support, Product, Legal
  • Monitoring dashboards (health, errors, conversion, retention) — DevOps, Engineering, Analytics
  • Incident response and escalation playbook — DevOps, Support, Engineering
  • Phased GA rollout plan (e.g., 10% → 50% → 100%) — Product, Engineering, Growth

Acceptance criteria for promotion

These questions must be answered before promoting the feature to the next environment.

Alpha → Beta

  • □ Are all critical and high-severity bugs resolved or documented with workarounds?
  • □ Has legal/compliance signed off on disclosures, flows, and regulatory requirements?
  • □ Does the end-to-end flow (goal → term → link bank → fund) complete successfully in staging?
  • □ Are analytics and tracking verified for funnel and conversion measurement?
  • □ Is the Beta cohort defined and the invitation/onboarding flow ready?

Beta → GA

  • □ Does Day-30 retention meet or exceed the target threshold?
  • □ Is Time-to-Value (TTV) within acceptable limits for the target segment?
  • □ Have we identified a winning messaging/value-prop variant from A/B tests?
  • □ Are support, monitoring, and incident response ready for production traffic?
  • □ Has load testing confirmed the system can handle expected GA volume?

"Keep it simple and keep it moving."

Examples reference apps that do each pattern well. Expand any concept to see multiple examples.

1

Progressive Disclosure

Surface primary info first; hide complexity until needed.
  1. Google Search — Single search box upfront; "Tools" and "Advanced Search" reveal filters only when needed. View →
  2. Chime / Robinhood — Break onboarding into small steps; delay KYC friction while front-loading value. Built for Mars ranks both for lowest onboarding friction. Built for Mars →
  3. Unit — Dynamic disclosures (e.g., interest rates) update automatically in terms; no re-acceptance needed. Unit docs →
  4. iwoca — Fintech sign-up uses progressive disclosure to manage expectations; one question at a time. iwoca →
2

One-Click Flows

Minimize steps to reduce friction and get users earning faster.
  1. Chime Quick Fund — One-tap "Add Money" on Savings screen; Checking → Savings pre-selected. Mirrors our "Add to annuity" pattern. Chime help →
  2. Acorns Round-Ups — Spare change invested automatically; Real-Time Round-Ups with every swipe. One-tap investing. Acorns →
  3. Cash App — Send money via $cashtag, phone, or email with minimal taps. Cash App →
  4. Stripe Checkout + Apple Pay — One tap completes payment; surfaces Apple Pay, Google Pay, Link automatically. Stripe docs →
3

Visual Progress Tracking

Show users where they are in the journey to build confidence and reduce drop-off.
  1. Duolingo — Progress bars, streak counters, XP bar; clear path shows exactly where you are. Gamification reinforces completion. Duolingo →
  2. Savings Pulse stepper — Goal → Term → Link → Fund → Connect → Done. Same pattern for our onboarding. View demo →
4

Direct Feedback

Immediate confirmation and clear next steps at every stage.
  1. Notion — Inline checkmarks, "Saved" toasts, clear next-step prompts. Users always know what just happened. Notion →
  2. Stripe — Success states, webhook confirmations, clear post-payment next steps. Stripe docs →
5

No Hidden Fees

What you see is what you get — no commissions, no surprises.
  1. Spruce — No monthly fees, no balance minimums, no foreign transaction fees. Short, explicit fee list (5 items). Spruce fees →
  2. MoneyLion — "100% transparency," low or zero fees, no hidden charges. MoneyLion →
  3. Loop — "Transparent FX rates and honest pricing with no fine print." Loop →
6

Grace for Occasional Dips

Encourage saving without punishing a bad month. Every month is a fresh start.
  1. Headspace — "Give Yourself Grace" meditation for bad days. No shame for missed streaks; "Every day is a fresh start." Headspace →
  2. Calm — In-the-moment exercises (Panic SOS, breathing) for acute stress; supportive without judgment. Calm →
  3. Savings Pulse — Month-over-month changes shown neutrally; supportive copy when savings dip. View demo →

In-app experience (post-funding)

Simplicity extends beyond onboarding. The ongoing in-app experience should make money flow and compounding impact instantly understandable.

Money in / money out — simplest graphical form

  • Single-view flow diagram: Income → Savings → Annuity (arrows, not tables)
  • Minimal labels: "In," "Out," "To annuity" — no jargon
  • Visual proportion (e.g., bar widths) reflects actual amounts
  • One-tap drill-down for details; default view stays simple
  • Month-over-month changes shown neutrally — avoid discouraging users when savings dip; supportive copy like "Some months you'll save more, some less — that's okay. Every month is a fresh start."

Compounding impact — show the benefit of acting sooner

  • Side-by-side comparison: "Add $X now" vs "Add $X in 6 months" — outcome at maturity
  • Single headline: "You'd have $X more by moving this to your annuity today"
  • Simple progress: "This month: $Y is earning. Next month: $Y + interest."
  • Avoid compound-interest formulas; show results, not math

Analytics & Event Tracking Plan

A structured approach to measuring success across the funnel. Events map to North Star, Engagement, and Efficiency metrics and support Alpha → Beta → GA decisions.

Two-Fold Segmentation: New vs Tenured

All events and metrics are tracked separately by customer segment to support distinct strategies and benchmarks.

New

First-time Savings Pulse users with no prior funded account. Focus: acquisition, onboarding friction, TTV, funnel conversion.

Tenured

Existing customers with a prior funded account or existing product relationship. Focus: engagement, add-to-annuity behavior, retention, incremental AUM.

Every event includes customer_segment: new | tenured. Dashboards and reports default to segment-filtered views.

Event Categories

Funnel

Goal set → Term selected → Bank linked → Funded → Connected

Engagement

Return visits, feature usage, add-to-annuity taps, money flow views

Business

Deposit amounts, AUM attribution, cohort revenue

Derived Metrics

All metrics calculated separately for new and tenured segments.

  • Time-to-Value (TTV) — Time from first touch (page load / CTA click) to deposit_confirmed
  • Funnel conversion rate — % completing each step (goal → term → link → fund → connect)
  • Day-30 retention — % of users who funded and returned within 30 days
  • Net new inflow — Sum of deposits attributed to Savings Pulse by cohort

Build Plan (Vertical Slicing)

Each slice delivers an end-to-end, testable increment — UI, API, and persistence — rather than building layer by layer. Slices prioritize in-app features first to add value for all customers: tenured users get dashboard and add-to-annuity immediately; new users gain the same once onboarding is built.

Vertical slice = one thin slice through the full stack (frontend → backend → data) that delivers a usable outcome.

1

Slice 1: Bank Link

Bank linking is likely already integrated elsewhere and can be reused. Confirm Plaid transaction data is flowing; wire into Savings Pulse flow if needed. User links bank; token stored.

Milestone: Transaction data confirmed flowing from Plaid; bank link available for Savings Pulse.

2

Slice 2: Connected Dashboard

Post-funding in-app view. Money flow visualization (spending vs. savings by month). Use existing/mock data for tenured customers; new customers see it once funded.

Milestone: Tenured and funded users see money flow; value delivered to all active customers.

3

Slice 3: Add to Annuity

One-tap deposit from dashboard. Add-to-annuity flow; compounding impact display ("$X today ≈ $Y by maturity"). Core engagement for tenured and new alike.

Milestone: Users can add funds from dashboard; compounding copy visible; incremental AUM flow enabled.

4

Slice 4: Goal + Term

User sets savings goal and selects term. UI stepper (steps 1–2), API endpoints, persistence. Onboarding for new customers.

Milestone: New users can complete goal and term selection; data persists.

5

Slice 5: Initial Funding

Deposit flow: confirm amount, initiate transfer. Integrate with payment/ACH provider. User funds account; onboarding completes.

Milestone: End-to-end onboarding (goal → term → link → fund) works; new users become funded and see dashboard.

6

Slice 6: Analytics + Production Readiness

Event tracking (per analytics plan), monitoring dashboards, error handling, compliance review. Load testing, support docs, incident playbook.

Milestone: Alpha-ready; analytics verified; go/no-go checklist complete.

Slice dependencies

1 (bank link) first — confirm Plaid data flow; foundation for money flow. 2 and 3 (in-app) deliver value to tenured and funded users. 4 → 5 (onboarding) for new customers. 6 runs in parallel where possible.
